### 1. The single goal is "best YouTube videos," not "best videos"

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, opening framing, p.1**

> "Your goal here is to make the best YOUTUBE videos possible. That's the number one goal of this production company. It's not to make the best-produced videos. Not to make the funniest videos. Not to make the best looking videos. Not the highest quality videos. It's to make the best YouTube videos possible."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production* (2023-2024 internal doc; leaked Sep 2024). Cited in [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Sep/15/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production/) and [Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/news/mrbeast-leaked-pdf-spills-secrets-how-to-be-successful-on-youtube/).

**Why it matters:** This is the load-bearing premise that justifies every other rule. "Best YouTube video" = the video that wins in YouTube's specific attention market, defined by CTR + retention + algorithm fit. "Quality" is subordinated to "platform fit."

**Recurrence:** Restated as a principle in his Lex Fridman 2023 appearance ("whatever makes the best video" — [PodcastNotes](https://podcastnotes.org/lex-fridman-podcast/mrbeast-future-of-youtube-twitter-tiktok-and-instagram-lex-fridman-podcast-351/)) and on Joe Rogan #1788 ("It's much easier to get 5,000,000 views on one video than to get 100,000 views on 50 videos").

**Practice correspondence:** Explains why he kills videos mid-production ("I have no problem killing a video even if we've spent a million dollars on it" — Joe Rogan #1788). Quality of a single asset, by his definition, is "would-it-win-on-YouTube," so any video that won't win is unfinishable by definition.

### 2. Only three metrics matter: CTR, AVD, AVP

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1 ("What Makes YouTube Videos Viral")**

> "The three metrics you guys need to care about is Click Thru Rate (CTR), Average View Duration (AVD), and Average View Percentage (AVP)."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. Quoted in [Daniel Scrivner summary](https://www.danielscrivner.com/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production-summary/).

Definitions from the same chapter:
- **CTR**: "how many people see our thumbnail in their feeds divided by how many click it"
- **AVD**: continuous viewing length (seconds watched)
- **AVP**: "the average percentage of a video that viewers watch"

Source: [SlideShare excerpt](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765), [ProTunesOne extraction](https://protunesone.com/blog/leaked-mrbeast-document-on-his-youtube-strategies/).

**First articulated:** Verbally as early as his 2017 "How To Get Views On YouTube" tweets; codified formally inside the handbook (written ~2023).

**Practice correspondence:** The entire video factory (thumbnail A/B testing 20+ variants, minute-by-minute retention plot reviewing, killing videos with bad expected CTR) is engineered around these three numbers. Everything else — production design, set, talent, narrative — is downstream.

### 3. The "I Spent 50 Hours in Ketchup" CTR canon (concrete > literal)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1**

> "CTR is what dictates what we do for videos. 'I Spent 50 Hours In My Front Yard' is lame, and you wouldn't click it. But you would click 'I Spent 50 Hours In Ketchup.'"
> "A simple way to up that CTR even more would be to title it 'I Survived' instead of 'I Spent.' That would add more intrigue and make it feel more extreme. In general, the more extreme, the better."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. Quoted in [Creator Handbook](https://www.creatorhandbook.net/leaked-document-allegedly-reveals-mrbeasts-secrets-to-youtube-success-the-key-takeaways/) and [Dexerto](https://www.dexerto.com/youtube/leaked-mrbeast-pdf-reveals-youtubers-secrets-to-video-success-2900841/).

**Mental model:** Same effort, 100x virality delta. Concrete absurdity (ketchup) beats abstract realism (front yard). "Extreme > moderate" is a tactical rule, not a vibe.

**Practice correspondence:** Title "I Survived 50 Hours In Solitary Confinement" (vs. "I Tried 50 Hours In Solitary"), "I Spent 50 Hours Buried Alive" (vs. "Buried For 50 Hours") — the verb/object choice in every recent title follows this rule.

### 4. The first minute is the most important minute (retention triage)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1**

> "The first minute is the most important minute of each video because that's when people click off the most."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. Quoted in [Passionfruit](https://passionfru.it/mrbeast-leaked-document-81151/).

Quantified benchmark from same chapter: losing **21M of 60M initial viewers** in the first minute is considered "reasonably good performance." (Source: [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2024/Sep/15/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production/).)

**Implications stated in doc:**
- Minute 0-1 = "match the clickbait expectations and front-load as much information" with "maximum amount of visuals, music, effects and quick scene changes"
- Goal of minute 1 = prove the thumbnail's promise is real, immediately.

**Practice correspondence:** Every MrBeast video opens by showing the contestants, the prize, and the stakes within 30-45 seconds — never with logo/intro/backstory. The "thumbnail must appear on screen within seconds" rule is enforced.

### 5. The minute-by-minute video structure (his own retention curve doctrine)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1 (paraphrased structure with verbatim phrases)**

| Segment | Rule (verbatim or near-verbatim) |
|---|---|
| 0-1 min | Front-load. "Match the clickbait expectations." Maximum visual density. |
| 1-3 min | Deploy **"crazy progression"** — compress multiple narrative beats, don't sequentially storytell. |
| 3 min mark | First **"re-engagement"**: spectacle "no other creator can do." |
| 3-6 min | Quick cuts, simple stimulating content. |
| 6 min mark | Second re-engagement to push viewer into back half. |
| Back half | "Lull" content — viewer is now emotionally invested, can sustain explanation, slower beats. |
| Ending | "End it abruptly and keep the energy high." |

Sources: [Daniel Scrivner extraction](https://www.danielscrivner.com/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production-summary/), [ProTunesOne](https://protunesone.com/blog/leaked-mrbeast-document-on-his-youtube-strategies/), [SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765).

Average MrBeast video length analyzed across 100 videos: **13m 37s / 81,801s total** (Daniel Scrivner).

**Note on "A1/A2/A3":** The user prompt mentioned an A1/A2/A3 video classification system. The leaked handbook in fact uses an **A-Player / B-Player / C-Player** *employee* classification (see #8 below) and a **minute-by-minute video segment** structure (this entry). No public surfacing of the handbook so far contains an A1/A2/A3 video taxonomy. Multiple Tier-2 syntheses ([Creator Handbook](https://www.creatorhandbook.net/leaked-document-allegedly-reveals-mrbeasts-secrets-to-youtube-success-the-key-takeaways/), [Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/news/mrbeast-leaked-pdf-spills-secrets-how-to-be-successful-on-youtube/)) explicitly confirm "no A1/A2/A3 classification system appears in this handbook." If the term exists, it is in unleaked internal slides or verbal team usage, not in any source surfaced as of 2026-05.

**Practice correspondence:** Watch retention plots for "$1 vs $100,000,000 Car!" or "Last To Leave Circle Wins $500,000" — the inflection bumps line up almost exactly with min-3 and min-6 re-engagement moments.

### 6. The "Wow Factor" doctrine (defensible competitive moat)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1**

> "Anything that no other youtuber can do… It changes how they see us."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production* on the Wow Factor. ([SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765))

Operationally: crane-delivered prizes, giving away private islands, buying entire stores. Each re-engagement beat (3-min, 6-min) is supposed to contain Wow.

**Why it matters:** This is his theory of moats. The reason viewers click MrBeast specifically (not just any high-CTR thumbnail) is because the audience has learned that MrBeast videos uniquely deliver something competitors *literally cannot afford or coordinate*. Every dollar reinvested (#11) is reinvested in raising this floor.

**Recurrence:** Stated again on Lex Fridman 2023 — "Going viral on YouTube is a learnable skill, it is not random" ([PodcastNotes](https://podcastnotes.org/lex-fridman-podcast/mrbeast-future-of-youtube-twitter-tiktok-and-instagram-lex-fridman-podcast-351/)). Wow Factor is the operationalization of "non-random virality."

### 7. Money must be visible on camera (capital allocation rule)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 2 ("Creating Content")**

> "I want money spent to be shown on camera ideally. If you're spending over $10,000 on something and it won't be shown on camera, seriously think about it."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. ([Daniel Scrivner](https://www.danielscrivner.com/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production-summary/), [SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765))

Paired rule from same chapter:

> "Creativity saves money."
> ($20,000 prize vs. a year's supply of Doritos costing $1,825 — the Doritos option produced equivalent or better viewer reaction.)

**Why it matters:** This is the inverse of typical Hollywood/agency thinking. Production overhead invisible to the viewer is waste. Every dollar should produce a frame of video that contributes to CTR/AVD/AVP.

**Practice correspondence:** "$1 vs $1,000,000,000 Yacht!" — almost all the budget is visible. Backstage logistics is intentionally minimal. The studio campus expansion he discussed with Colin & Samir is justified by *enabling on-camera spectacle*, not by comfort.

### 9. Communication hierarchy + the dependency / bottleneck framework

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 2**

> "Ideally when communicating across departments you go up and then over."
> Communication ranked: in-person > phone call > text > email (email is "the lowest form").
> "Written communication doesn't count unless confirmed read."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. ([SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765), [Daniel Scrivner](https://www.danielscrivner.com/how-to-succeed-in-mrbeast-production-summary/))

Bottleneck protocol (paraphrased from handbook, verbatim fragments):
> "Tyler, you are my bottleneck." (Example template.)
> Identify the specific blocker → tell them directly → set a date → check daily.

Critical Components rule:
> "WITHOUT WHAT YOU'RE WORKING ON WE DO NOT HAVE A VIDEO!"
> "Protect it at all costs, check in on it 10x a day."
> — ibid.

**Why it matters:** This is a production engineering discipline borrowed from constraint-theory operations management, ported into creator-economy execution. Every video has a tiny number of "critical components" (e.g., a secured island, a permit, a specific celebrity slot); if any breaks, no video.

**Practice correspondence:** The 400-day/$4M production of recent megavideos ([GamerRant](https://gamerant.com/mrbeast-new-video-2025/)) is only tractable because the critical-component spine is identified and protected with daily check-ins.

### 10. "No Does Not Mean No"

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 2/3**

> "No does not mean no."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. ([Creator Handbook](https://www.creatorhandbook.net/leaked-document-allegedly-reveals-mrbeasts-secrets-to-youtube-success-the-key-takeaways/), [Gist summary](https://gist.github.com/cognitivetech/55870132c63d8f456f8b550d0d6127a3))

Doctrine: when an external party (location owner, brand, government, supplier) refuses, that's the *start* of the negotiation, not the end. Exhaust alternative framings, alternative budgets, alternative parties before accepting rejection.

**Practice correspondence:** Filming inside actual prisons, on actual aircraft carriers, in active volcanoes — these are normally hard "no"s for any creator. The handbook teaches the team to treat the first "no" as procedural noise.

### 11. Reinvest 100% — the compounding loop

**TIER 1 — JRE #1788 (Feb 2022), MrBeast verbatim**

> "It shouldn't be revolutionary that I reinvest all my money; like that's something businesses have been doing for centuries."
> "I think living your life chasing like a nicer and nicer car, and a bigger and bigger box to live in is kind of like a dumb way to go about life."
> "I have no problem killing a video even if we've spent a million dollars on it."

> "Instead of thinking about building my own platform, I can think about building businesses and leveraging my audience off of YouTube."
> — ibid.

**Mental model:** Every dollar earned → reinvested into bigger production budget → produces a more extreme Wow Factor video → produces more views → produces more revenue → loop. The loop only breaks if (a) he stops reinvesting or (b) extracts for personal lifestyle. Both prohibited.

### 13. The 2024 pivot: slower pacing, storytelling, longer videos

**TIER 1 — MrBeast's own tweet, Mar 3, 2024**

> "This past year i've slowed down our videos, focused on story telling, let scenes breathe, yelled less, more personality, longer videos, etc. And our views have skyrocketed! My fellow YouTubers lets get rid of the ultra fast paced/overstim era of content. It doesn't even work."
> — @MrBeast, Mar 3, 2024. <https://x.com/MrBeast/status/1764293361437909406>

**Why it's load-bearing:** This is MrBeast publicly *contradicting* the editing style he himself popularized (rapid cuts every 1-2 seconds, constant shouting, no breath). It signals an updated retention model: at his audience scale, AVD on longer videos > AVP on short ones, because the algorithm rewards total watch-time. Storytelling is now a retention tool, not an aesthetic preference.

**Recurrence:** Confirmed in production analyses ([Tubefilter](https://www.tubefilter.com/2024/03/04/mrbeast-editing-style-number-of-cuts-per-video/), [PassionFruit](https://passionfru.it/youtube-editing-mrbeast-54202/)). Reinforced on Diary of a CEO (Feb 2025): storytelling and tension-building now central to his retention doctrine ([Mercedes Chloe synthesis](https://mercedeschloe.com/mrbeast-on-the-diary-of-a-ceo-the-blueprint-behind-youtubes-biggest-star/)).

**Practice correspondence:** Compare a 2020 video (e.g. "Last To Take Hand Off Lamborghini") with 2024-2025 videos like "Beast Games" episodes — the cut frequency dropped, run-time increased to 15-25+ minutes, narrative arcs got actual second-act emotional beats.

### 14. One great video > many mediocre videos (evergreen compounding)

**TIER 1 — JRE #1788 + Lex Fridman #351**

> "It's much easier to get 5,000,000 views on one video than to get 100,000 views on 50 videos."
> — MrBeast on JRE #1788. ([PodcastNotes](https://podcastnotes.org/joe-rogan-experience/1788-mr-beast-joe-rogan-experience/))

> "Unique content may sacrifice views short-term, but if it eventually wins, it wins more."
> — MrBeast on Lex Fridman #351. ([PodcastNotes](https://podcastnotes.org/lex-fridman-podcast/mrbeast-future-of-youtube-twitter-tiktok-and-instagram-lex-fridman-podcast-351/))

**Why it matters:** Direct rebuttal to the "post daily / volume game" creator strategy. He explicitly argues that the YouTube algorithm rewards exceptional individual assets that become evergreen (re-recommended for years), and that production budget concentrated into one hit beats the same budget diluted across many.

**Practice correspondence:** He posts ~1 main-channel video per month. Each is engineered as a potential evergreen monster.

### 15. Brand deals are content (not interruption)

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 3**

> "BRAND DEALS ARE CONTENT!"
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. ([SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765))

Doctrine: a sponsor mention is not a tax viewers tolerate to fund the content — it should be *integrated as a video beat* that maintains or increases retention. If a brand integration causes a retention dip, it's a failure of integration, not an inherent cost.

**Practice correspondence:** Honey/HelloFresh/Shopify reads in MrBeast videos are converted into contest mechanics, prize fulfillment, or set pieces — never separated talking-head cutaways. This is also why he started building owned brands (Feastables, MrBeast Burger) — to be the integration rather than carrying others'.

### 18. Beast Philanthropy: charity-as-entertainment by design

**TIER 1 — Beast Philanthropy official site**

> "We donate 100% of the revenue generated from our content to charity!"
> "Beast Philanthropy aims to make the world a better place by harnessing social media's power."
> — [beastphilanthropy.org/who-we-are](https://www.beastphilanthropy.org/who-we-are)

**TIER 2 — Confirmed model:** Head of Beast Philanthropy, Darren Margolias, calls the format "philanthro-tainment" — videos engineered to be entertaining first so that they reach the broadest possible audience, which is how the charity scales ([Opp Institutions analysis](https://www.oppinstitutions.org/mrbeast-and-the-problem-of-philanthropy-as-spectacle/), [Why Philanthropy Matters](https://whyphilanthropymatters.com/article/good-for-ratings-mrbeast-philanthropy-in-the-attention-economy/)).

**MrBeast's own framing (Lex Fridman 2023):**
> "Hopefully there is a day where I can give away a billion dollars in a video."

### 19. Authenticity = retention (not a moral stance)

### 20. No dull moments + abrupt ending

**TIER 1 — Leaked handbook, Chapter 1/3**

> "Hook people at the start, transition them to amazing story, have no dull moments, satisfying payoff, abrupt ending."
> — *How to Succeed in MrBeast Production*. ([SlideShare](https://www.slideshare.net/slideshow/how-to-succeed-at-mrbeast-production-handbook/271808765))

The "abrupt ending" rule is counterintuitive — most TV teaches a wind-down. MrBeast argues: as soon as the payoff resolves, *cut*. Don't let viewers click off voluntarily; you want them to finish at peak energy so AVP stays maxed.

**Practice correspondence:** MrBeast videos rarely have outros, talking-head sign-offs, or "subscribe" calls at the end. The final beat is typically the prize reveal, then black-frame within 3-5 seconds.
